125th Anniversary & Pharmacy Building Reopening
October 6, 2017

On Friday, October 6th, the Ernest Mario School of Pharmacy celebrated the grand opening of the new-and-improved pharmacy school. The add-on to the original building is full of well-lit, open spaces and beautiful glass sculptures. After the ribbon-cutting, everybody was welcome to enjoy the delicious selection of food and drink. It was a classy affair, complete with a wine bar, multiple entree stations, and a dessert stand.

After everybody had a chance to eat, Beta Nu gathered by the John L. Colaizzi Atrium for a group picture and then a tour of the new school led by P3 Brother Nikhil Dondapati and P1 Brother Daniel Chen. Some highlights of the new classrooms include a room full of laminar flow hoods for lab, a simulation room with a blinking and breathing dummy for interprofessional training, and patient assessment rooms that make you feel like you are at your doctor's office - complete with the examination chair and a blood pressure cuff attached to the wall. Alumni and collegiate brothers alike had a good time exploring the new school together. It was a wonderful reunion to celebrate the school that brought us all together.
